Wanderlust

Track your travels

Wanderlust allows world travelers to track their country or state visits using a D3 interactive map. This map enables users to click on a country or state. Once the country or state color has been changed, the visits are stored for future use. A user has the ability to upload a photo, which then becomes a postcard. The front displays a picture, while the back displays the address and description. Wanderlust also provides users with a timeline in which they can add their photos and journal their travels. This app allows users to track all their travels in one convenient place. Discover new and exciting places and track all your world travels with Wanderlust.

Stack: Pyton, JavaScript / jQuery, Bootstrap, HTML5, CSS3, D3.js

  • Client: Uma Petersen
  • Date: December 2015
  • Service: Web Development

Democracy Tuesday

Newsletter to empower users with update current news

What is the purpose:

The concept of Democracy Tuesday is to empower individuals to stay updated with current news. Finding creditable news sources is a significant issue today. We are targeting young professionals in hopes the newsletter provides a quick read to insightful news on current events and politics. The newsletter will engage users in a quick read and also allow them to take action in their own local communities.

Problem trying to solve:

Client’s goal is to reach out to as many users as possible who are interested in learning and participating in current politics. The concept is to allow them to gain insight and exposure to current topics while to staying up- to-date and informed. Current newsletter is text email where look and feel do not convey client’s excitement about user’s engagement in politics.

Goals

• need a way to reach out to new and current users • need a way to interest more users with an attractive interface • desire a customized logo and banner

Scenario:

Reading the news as you go to work • user is on public transit • user checks emails • user clicks Democracy Tuesday • user has a quick read of content • user can choose to continue to read or click other content

Sketching Interactions:

Based on the concept and user scenarios, I then sketched the interface interactions. The sketch was of three different email designs. The sketches take into account each platform for which I am designing the newsletter : IOS, Andriod, and Web

Wireframes:

Research: Theme and Vision Board

After research on newsletter as well as gathering information about client’s needs as well as the users I created a vision board to to illustrate theme, look and feel of the newsletter. I created wireframes that detail where the elements and content should be placed on each Mobile and Desktop Wireframe

Mockups: First iteration

I incorporated the client’s needs into two variations of the newsletter. In the first mockup I used the clients colors. In the second mock-up I chose colors I thought would help to instantly inform users of the newsletter content.

Mockups: Second Iteration

Changed the mockup to new colors and I added header image and subscribe button at the top

Mockups: Third iteration

Removed header image and added grey boxes around text to highlight its content

Mockups: Final iteration

After presenting two clients decided they wanted to add back the header image for the final draft.
